FINDING 1: Direction accuracy values — manuscript vs all_metrics.csv
================================================================================

Codex computed (from all_metrics.csv, mean-of-dataset-means):
  ssGSEA 0.733, GSVA 0.705, zscore 0.773, AUCell 0.605, UCell 0.662

Manuscript claims (from v2_corrected_results.txt):
  ssGSEA 0.648, GSVA 0.614, zscore 0.694, AUCell 0.538, UCell 0.555

VERDICT: BOTH ARE TECHNICALLY CORRECT — they use different aggregations.

ROOT CAUSE: The R script that generated v2_corrected_results.txt used:
  aggregate(direction_correct ~ method, data=sub, FUN=mean)
This POOLS all 9,320 individual comparisons, giving CKD kidney's 1,360
comparisons 34x the weight of DCM heart's 40 comparisons.

all_metrics.csv stores per-dataset direction accuracy, so averaging gives
MEAN-OF-DATASET-MEANS (each dataset weighted equally).

After ground-truth correction, DCM jumped from 35% to 87.5% (4 wrong
pathways removed), but DCM has only 40 comparisons. In the pooled mean
this barely moves the needle (+0.3% per method). In the mean-of-means
it raises every method by 6-10%.

WHAT TO DO: The manuscript should use mean-of-dataset-means (standard in
benchmarks, used by Soneson & Robinson 2018, Saelens et al. 2019). The
correct values are those in all_metrics.csv:
  ssGSEA 0.733, GSVA 0.705, zscore 0.773, AUCell 0.605, UCell 0.662

The manuscript's v2_corrected_results.txt summary table is INCONSISTENT
with its own per-dataset table (which shows the correct per-dataset values
that average to 0.733, not 0.648).

FINDING 2: AUCell scenario D — positive (+0.054) or negative (-0.27)?
================================================================================

Codex found: AUCell scenario D mean d = +0.054 from simulation CSV.
Manuscript claims: AUCell "flips sign" to d ~ -0.27.

VERDICT: THE CHECK IS CORRECT. The manuscript value is from a SINGLE
unreplicated simulation run. The 100-replicate CSV shows:

  AUCell scenario D: mean d = +0.054, SD = 0.248
  40 of 100 replicates are negative (40% sign inversion rate)
  Range: [-0.528, +0.588]

The mean is slightly POSITIVE, not negative. AUCell does NOT reliably
flip sign — it becomes NOISY and uninformative (d ≈ 0 ± 0.25), with
sign inversion in 40% of replicates. This is still a meaningful failure
(a method scoring d ≈ 0 when the true effect is d ≈ 9 for zscore) but
the manuscript's "flips to -0.27" overstates the finding.

WHAT TO DO: Revise the manuscript text from:
  "AUCell's Cohen's d flipped to -0.27"
to:
  "AUCell's Cohen's d collapsed to near zero (0.054 ± 0.25, with 40%
   of replicates showing sign inversion), while magnitude-based methods
   maintained strong detection (Z-score d = 9.4 ± 0.9)"

The qualitative finding (AUCell fails under competition) is preserved;
only the quantitative claim changes.
